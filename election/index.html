2020 BOARD OF DIRECTORS ELECTION

By resolution of the Board of Directors in response to the coronavirus pandemic, the 2020 Board of Directors election will take place using our action by written ballot without a meeting procedures.

Bylaws, Article 1, Section 9, subsection D.

Here's a timeline of the election by written ballot without a meeting process:

July 18th to August 15th
Nomination period for director candidates begins with the July Board meeting
Members in good standing may submit BOD nominations to nominations@sfpride.org
July 18th to Aug 23rd
Corporation staff verify mailing address of members
August 23rd though September 19th:
Corporation staff mail out written ballots no later than Aug 23rd.
Ballots may be returned by self-addressed pre-stamped enveloped provided in mailing

Dame Maggie Smith
she/her/hers

Dame Margaret Natalie Smith CH DBE (born 28 December 1934) is an English actress. She has had an extensive career on stage, film, and television which began in the mid-1950s. Smith has appeared in more than 60 films and over 70 plays, and is one of Britain's most recognisable actresses. She was made a Dame by Queen Elizabeth II in 1990 for contributions to the performing arts,[1] and a Companion of Honour in 2014 for services to drama.

Smith began her career on stage as a student, performing at the Oxford Playhouse in 1952, and made her professional debut on Broadway in New Faces of '56. For her work on the London stage, she has won a record six Best Actress Evening Standard Awards: for The Private Ear, and The Public Eye (both 1962), Hedda Gabler (1970), Virginia (1981), The Way of the World (1984), Three Tall Women (1994) and A German Life (2019). She received Tony Award nominations for Private Lives (1975) and Night and Day (1979), before winning the 1990 Tony Award for Best Actress in a Play for Lettice and Lovage. She appeared in Stratford Shakespeare Festival productions of Antony and Cleopatra (1976) and Macbeth (1978), and West End productions of A Delicate Balance (1997) and The Breath of Life (2002). She received the Society of London Theatre Special Award in 2010.
